How Marblehead's total property tax levy grows over 30 years

Marblehead collects roughly $84.9 million in property taxes today. Under Proposition 2½, that entire levy automatically grows 2.5% every year whether or not any override passes. Each override scenario layers new permanent capacity on top of that baseline, and that new capacity compounds at 2.5% too. Figures show the town's total annual property tax levy in $ millions, with the increase above today's baseline shown below each total.

Even under "No overrides," the levy roughly doubles by year 30 through compounding alone. Any override authorization becomes a permanent, compounding addition on top of that growth and Town Meeting is not legally bound to spend new capacity on the purposes it was approved for.

The compounding story: Even if both override questions fail, Marblehead's total property tax levy still grows from $84.9 million today to $173.7 million by year 30, a permanent increase of $88.8 million, or more than double today's collections, created entirely by Proposition 2½'s automatic 2.5% annual escalator. Under Tier 3 + Trash, the levy reaches $209.1 million, with $35.4 million of that coming from the override base compounding on top of whatever voters authorize.
Why the baseline matters: Override debates typically focus on the new amount on the ballot ($9M, $12M or $15M plus $2.3M trash). But Marblehead's levy grows every year regardless and that growth, like any override, is permanent and compounding. Town Meeting decides appropriations each year; new levy capacity, once authorized, is not legally tied to the purposes it was approved for.
Baseline of $84.9M reflects Marblehead's approved FY2026 total property tax levy. Override base amounts from the April 8, 2026 town override presentation. Year 1 reflects the baseline plus initial override authorization; years 10, 20 and 30 apply the 2.5% annual Proposition 2½ growth factor compounded on both the baseline and override base. Delta below each total shows the increase above today's baseline, including baseline compounding and any override contribution.
